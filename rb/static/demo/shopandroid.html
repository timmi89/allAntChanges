Product Description

The iOttie Easy One Touch 2 provides a safe, versatile and highly functional smartphone mounting solution!

Like its predecessors, it's packed with standard features like the iOttie patented one touch locking feature as well as the super sticky gel pad. In addition they've designed a new two-step locking lever which ensures your mount can be used on different surfaces.

The first locking position can be used for flat, smooth surfaces while the second locking position can be used on curvy textured surfaces that may require more grip. The One Touch 2 also offers a telescopic arm which extends up to 2 additional inches to provide you with even more viewing options when using your mount.

The redesigned bottom foot ensures that there is no interference with your devices bottom ports. The stickiness of the suction cup is infinite, just rinse with warm water and let air dry, restoring the gel pad to like new condition.

No matter if your phone is protected by a skin or a case, the iOttie Easy One Touch 2 Car Mount grips securely onto your device so you can drive with confidence. Mount Smart. Drive Smarter!

Features:

  • Holds devices with widths of 2.3-3.2 inches
  • Two step locking lever provides additional mounting support for multiple surfaces
  • New telescopic arm adds 2 inches to allow for closer device viewing
  • Super sticky gel pad sticks securely to most surfaces (including textured surfaces)
  • Bottom foot ensures access to all your device ports
  • Easy one touch mounting system
  • Fully adjustable with 360 degree rotation for quick portrait and landscape views

iOttie Easy One Touch 2 Car Mount review
Reviewed by Ara Wagoner
The iOttie Easy One Touch 2 Car Mount is quite a mouthful. Its also quite a handful when you pick it up, as it resembles some sort of unholy union between a medieval torture device and a siege engine. Dont get me wrong, the iOttie is a wonderful mount with a lot of bells, whistles, and especially a lot of moving parts. Its also sporting a budget friendly price, but do all those moving parts add up to a stand thats worthy of your dashboard or a massive mechanical migraine?

If you havent gotten yourself a car mount yet, you should really consider making the investment. A great and growing number of communities are passing legislation against holding a phone while driving, meaning that if you need to follow Google Maps, youll either be needing a mount or looking down a lot at the phone in your cupholder assuming your shrinking cupholders are big enough for todays growing smartphones. As a Moto X user, I can tell you that Moto Voice and all voice commands work better with the phone mounted in front of me rather than shouting it down at the console, cupholder, or worst of all, my pocket.
