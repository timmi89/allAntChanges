In The 5 Years Since Lehman Brothers Collapsed What's Changed?

  • 09/15/2013 03:39 PM EDT U.S.

5 years ago this weekend, Lehman Brothers collapsed, marking the low point of the 2008 recession. Millions of people lost their jobs, unemployment skyrocketed, and a web of shady financial practices were revealed. 
 
Now, half a decade later, as the US economy continues its slow recovery, the anniversary is prompting reflection on what went wrong and how it could have been prevented.
