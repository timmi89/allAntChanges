There Have Been Plenty of Gay Senators, But How About an Openly Gay One?

Posted September 6th 1:30pm by HVpolitics
Read More: Tags: Health Care, Human Rights, Jeff Fitzgerald, LGBT, Madison, Ron Kind, Sen. Herb Kohl, Steve Kagen, Tammy Baldwin, Tommy Thompson, U.S. Senate, Wisconsin

email

She’s out. And she’s in. There have been plenty of gay senators working tirelessly against the interests of the LGBT community. Rep. Tammy Baldwin on Tuesday threw her hat in the ring to become the nation’s first openly gay senator, where she’d likely work tirelessly for the interests of that same community.

But Baldwin, smartly, doesn’t want to represent a small slice of the Wisconsin and American populations: She pledges to be a champion of the middle class, focusing on economic issues, not cultural issues.

“People are asking, ‘Doesn’t anyone hear us?’ I do, and that’s why I’m running for United States Senate,” she told reporters on a half-hour conference call.

Baldwin had been widely rumored to join the race, and the seven-term congresswoman who represents liberal Madison immediately became the front-runner for the Democratic nomination.

Democratic Rep. Ron Kind and former Rep. Steve Kagen are also rumored to be want into the contest for Sen. Herb Kohl’s soon-to-be-vacated seat. Kohl announced that he will not seek re-election after four terms in the U.S. Senate, and there would be something interesting about an openly gay politician taking over for a man who has been dogged by rumors of his own sexuality for many years.

On the Republican side, former Republican Rep. Mark Neumann has already entered the race. Neumann may face former Gov. Tommy Thompson and Assembly Speaker Jeff Fitzgerald in the GOP primary.

If you don’t care about the whole “She’s cool ‘cuz she’s gay!” thing (not that there’s anything wrong with that), here are some tidbits on the congresswoman: In 2010, National Journal ranked her in a tie at the top for most liberal member of Congress. She voted against the authorization of the invasion of Iraq in 2002. She co-sponsored a 2007 bill, H. Res. 333, that proposed articles of impeachment against VP Dick Cheney, as well as H Res. 589, which offered the same to Attorney General Alberto Gonzales. In the last Congress, she authored three health-care bills that would expand coverage to low-income women, military veterans and people with paralysis. See more positions here.
