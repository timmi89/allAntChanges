    LIGHTWEIGHT FLEX WITH MAXIMUM BREATHABILITY


    The Nike Free SB Men’s Skateboarding Shoe combines a lightweight mesh upper with the natural flexibility of Nike Free technology and the durable performance of a board shoe for the ultimate in comfort and control.


    Lightweight Breathability

    The upper is made of comfortable suede and mesh panels that allow ventilation to keep your feet cool and dry.


    Board Grip and Balance

    The skate-specific sole has Nike Free flex grooves for a comfortable, natural feel and better board grip and balance. Rubber outsole pods provide extra traction and control.


    More Benefits

  • Reflective elements help keep you visible in low light
  • Herringbone pattern on the outsole for traction in all directions
  • Phylon foam under the heel for lightweight cushioning Padded tongue for comfort

    • Nike Free Origins

    After learning that Stanford athletes had been training barefoot on the university’s golf course, three of Nike’s most innovative and creative employees set out to develop a shoe that felt natural and weightless, similar to bare feet. In 2002, they examined a group of men and women with pressure-measuring insoles taped to their feet, using high-speed cameras to capture images of each foot in motion. The team spent eight years studying the biomechanics of shoeless running. The results yielded a profound understanding of the foot’s natural landing angle, pressure and toe position, allowing Nike designers to build an unconventional and flexible running shoe from the inside out.

    Just not durable
    Sheialnl Sacramento, CA, USA , I run: 0-15 miles a week, I run primarily to: Stay in shape

    It's an amazing shoe that I wanted to like so badly. I used these shoes only when skating and use an etcetera insole. I have purchased 2 pairs and they each lasted less than 20 hours of skating each. The main two problems I encountered were the sole separating at the toe and the tread on each of the pads under the toes falling off. The shoe feels great, and is really lightweight it just isn't strong enough for skateboarding. The sole separation if caught quickly can be fixed with shoegoo but the tread peeling off the soul is unacceptable especially for the price of this shoe. If this shoe was $20 dollars it still would not be worth the time because it would have fallen apart before it even has time to break in. I would and probably will buy the updated model of this shoe provided Nike go back to drawing board and toughen up the materials. A+ performance but this is a skate shoe and has to be built to withstand excessive abuse.
