Team Antenna


Porter Bayne

Before moving back to NYC in June 2010, Porter launched two angel-funded startups in Seattle - more or less at the same time. Nearlyweds, which he sits on the board of, acquired OneWed.com in Fall 2010 and is poised to bring much needed innovation to the intersection of online bridal technology, media, and vendor recommendations. He also launched Ameritocracy in Spring 2008, which sought to address many similar problems and opportunities with online engagement as Antenna does. Ameritocracy closed in August 2010, and from the lessons learned there, Antenna was born.
Exemplifying the "will, not skill" mantra of startups, Porter is an expert at surrounding himself with people notably cleverer than he (in particular, the others shown here) - which, he likes to think, isn't as quite easy as it sounds.
His earlier career includes designing & building online software for Verizon Wireless, TV Guide, Zagat, aura readers, and defense contractors.
 

Eric Chaves

Eric moved from San Francisco 2 years ago to establish roots in New York and to start a company that will evolve how we respond to content on the web.
Eric is a web designer and developer. After graduating from Dartmouth in 2005, he moved to San Francisco where he worked in architecture and product design before later building a web development freelance business. Under the branding "thinksketchdesign" he designed user interfaces and built custom websites for small businesses.
Since moving to New York, he has been active in the tech community doing contract work for The Daily and CNNMoney. He currently works as a software developer for ZocDoc.

Mike is a very smart and multi-talented human. He is a killer systems engineer and developer for Antenna. As soon as he's not too busy keeping our servers up and running like champs, we'll ask him for a better bio.
