About ReadrBoard

A reaction button for the web.

ReadrBoard was founded by Eric, Tyler, and Porter, and our goal is to make it super easy for readers to say how they feel about whatever content online is really spurring their interest or their reaction.


Porter Bayne
Before moving back to NYC in June 2010, Porter launched two angel-funded startups in Seattle - more or less at the same time. Nearlyweds, which he sits on the board of, acquired OneWed.com in Fall 2010 and is poised to bring much needed innovation to the intersection of online bridal technology, media, and vendor recommendations. He also launched Ameritocracy in Spring 2008, which sought to address many similar problems and opportunities with online engagement as ReadrBoard does. Ameritocracy closed in August 2010, and from the lessons learned there, ReadrBoard was born.
Exemplifying the "will, not skill" mantra of startups, Porter is an expert at surrounding himself with people notably cleverer than he (in particular, the others shown here) - which, he likes to think, isn't as quite easy as it sounds.
His earlier career includes designing & building online software for Verizon Wireless, TV Guide, Zagat, aura readers, and defense contractors.
 
Tyler Brock
Tyler graduated from Duke University in 2007 with degrees in Economics and Computer Science as well as a certificate in Markets and Management. He was awarded distinction in Computer Science for his work on a database research project in conjunction with the Duke biology department.
Since returning home to New York, Tyler has worked in the finance industry, first at BlackRock and more recently as Vice President of Trading & Research at Harvest Volatility Management. While at Harvest, Tyler played a major role in developing and executing a trading strategy which lead to the fund raising over $1 billion AUM in the course of three years.
The kind of person that enjoys the routing of packets and the hum of a disk, Tyler is as passionate about digital interactions as the technology behind them.
 
Eric Chaves
Eric moved from San Francisco a year ago to establish roots in New York and to start a company that will evolve how we respond to content on the web. Luckily, he met Porter and Tyler who were eagerly working on the same problem. They've been working on ReadrBoard together since.
Eric is a web designer and developer. After graduating from Dartmouth in 2005, he moved to San Francisco where he worked in architecture and product design before later building a web development freelance business. Under the branding "thinksketchdesign" he designed user interfaces and built custom websites for small businesses.
Since moving to New York, he has continued work as a full-time freelancer while bootstrapping ReadrBoard. He worked as a design tech for The Daily as it was launching its iPad newspaper, and he currently works for CNNMoney as a webmaster.
