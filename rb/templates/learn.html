The future web will be an annotated web.

Readers want to say something specific, about something specific -- whether citing sources in a policy debate or reacting “LOL!” to a cat pic. That’s why forwarding-thinking sites like Medium, Quartz, and RapGenius are experimenting with inline comments.

But it’s more than just annotation. ReadrBoard enables understanding how different people and communities feel about the same content on the web. Our goal is to be THE tool people use to react to everything.

ReadrBoard is the best annotation platform ever made.

OK, that sounds ridiculous. We said it to ourselves as a joke. But then we remembered: no annotation platform has actually caught on outside of a single community.

ReadrBoard is different from others in two key ways.

First, reacting is simply easier than commenting. Sure, we let readers also leave a full, in-line comment if they want, but over 98% of people find a reaction does the trick.

Second, we enable responding to images and video, too!

Oh, and a third thing: we’re a platform. A free one. You can install ReadrBoard right now.

Since ReadrBoard’s getting used on 15% of pageviews for our partners -- and sometimes over 200% -- we think that’s starting to “catch on”. Maybe it is the best annotation platform ever made?

Try it out, and give us your feedback. This a process, and we want you to be a part of it. With your help, we can make the web more dynamic, and more aware of how people think and feel about great content.
